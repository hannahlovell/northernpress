Judge To Decide How Much Pharmacies Owe Over Opioid Crisis

(AP) In federal court in Cleveland, a judge will decide how much CVS, Walgreens, and Walmart pharmacies should pay two Ohio counties to help them with opioid-related expenditures and difficulties. A jury found pharmacy chains guilty for carelessly dispensing pain medicines in Lake and Trumbull counties. First time US pharmacies were blamed for opioid problem.Before trial, plaintiff's counsel argued each county required $1 billion to restore harm from the deluge of pills, which caused hundreds of overdose deaths.

Between 2012 and 2016, Trumbull County dispensed 80 million painkillers, or 400 per citizen, while Lake County dispensed 61 million, or 265 per resident. Dr. Katherine Keyes, a Columbia University epidemiologist, estimated that approximately 6,000 persons were addicted to opioids in Lake County in 2019 and nearly 7,600 in Trumbull County. Keyes found that many of children in the two counties had mental illness, learning impairments, and PTSD because their parents use illicit opiates.

Synthetic opioids like fentanyl have boosted drug overdose deaths in the two counties since 2015, Keyes said. Some users were addicted to prescription opioids first. She tested whether children of illegal opioid users were at risk for addiction.Attorneys for pharmacy chains questioned Keyes' technique.

In November, a jury agreed with the counties that the manner pharmacists administered pain medication created a public nuisance. Now, the counties will offer testimony from doctors about the harm to those communities, the opioid crisis' impact on child welfare and other county agencies, and an abatement strategy.

Mark Lanier, the counties' main attorney, said the jury's ruling should be heard in all pharmacies throughout America.Many lawsuits filed by governments over the medications' toll have been settled or won in recent years. According to the Associated Press, medicine makers, distributors, and pharmacies have settled for nearly $40 billion.
