Klonopin overdose

Large doses of Klonopin can cause hallucinations. No matter the user's intent, overdosing on this potent drug can be fatal. In high doses, it can slow the heart rate and impair breathing, causing coma or death.

Klonopin overdose symptoms: Fatigue shaky legs Dementia Poor focus Coordination Sloppiness

Paradoxical reactions are another side effect of long-term drug abuse. These are the opposite of what the drug was supposed to treat.

Here are some Klonopin abuse symptoms: Unrest Agitation agitation Worry Depression Asthma

Long-term use of low dose Klonopin can cause dependence and tolerance, and many patients become addicted despite following their doctor's prescription. The drug blocks brain receptors that regulate relaxation, stress, and anxiety. When dependence develops, a patient can't function without the drug and may experience withdrawal. Tolerance can lead to higher doses as patients no longer feel its effect at lower doses. As Klonopin dependence can develop in a month, patients should seek medical help when stopping use.

Klonopin drug combos

Polydrug use is common with Klonopin. People use other drugs to boost or mask clonazepam side effects. Some users mix Klonopin with stimulants like cocaine to increase their high or help them sleep. Cocaine suppresses Klonopin's effects, causing users to take more pills without realizing they could overdose. Cocaine has a shorter half-life than Klonopin, requiring more to maintain the desired high. Klonopin and other CNS depressants, such as alcohol, can cause fatal overdose. Combining alcohol and Klonopin can slow a user's CNS to the point where they stop breathing. No matter the combination, lethal overdose and serious side effects can have dire consequences.

A Klonopin Addiction The wide availability of Klonopin raises the risk of addiction. Some prescription users don't realize they have a dependency. This drug can alter brain neurochemistry with long-term use. Changes cause physical and mental dependency. However, not all drug abusers develop an addiction.

Signs of Klonopin addiction: Managing withdrawal with Klonopin Klonopin is hard to get. cravings for Klonopin Hazardous behavior Trying unsuccessfully to cut use ignoring duties Rx shopping Pharmacy fraud Off-label Klonopin

Abuse and addiction to Klonopin (Clonazepam) Recognizing an addiction to this drug is difficult because users often hide their medication from others. Addiction to this drug can cause physical, behavioral, and mental symptoms.

The Risks of Klonopin Seizures and fogginess are drug side effects. Though less potent than Halcon and Xanax, Klonopin is still addictive. Suicidal thoughts are another severe side effect of misusing this medication. 1 in 500 Klonopin users have suicidal thoughts, per the FDA. These statistics don't include Klonopin users without prescriptions. A Klonopin Intervention

An intervention is the best way to help patients understand their habit's consequences. The addict may feel confused or foggy during the intervention. Heavy users may be sleepy or nod off. It is important to conduct the intervention when the person is not on medication. When done correctly, an intervention can have a positive and effective impact on the user, so hiring a professional can be ideal. These experts know the right tone, words, and timing to use with a patient. What is Klonopin withdrawal? Klonopin boosts GABA, a calming brain chemical. GABA slows nerve signals and relaxes the body and mind. Long-term Klonopin use stops the brain from making its own chemicals. The brain overreacts when Klonopin is stopped because it lacks a necessary chemical. Known as withdrawal. Addicts who quit Klonopin on their own experience anxiety and nausea.
